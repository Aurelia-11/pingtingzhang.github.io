🔬 Research Experience

Materials

Thin-layered 2M WS₂

Synthesis: Prepared large-area (>20 μm) WS₂ with controlled layer numbers via optimised mechanical exfoliation, achieving reproducible thin-layered structures, and fabricated wafer-scale monolayer MoS₂ by CVD.

Stability: Through systematic investigation (Raman, XRD, TEM, AFM) and theoretical simulation of the phase transition behaviour of 2M WS₂, demonstrated that ultrathin 2M WS₂ possesses significantly higher intrinsic thermal stability than its bulk counterparts.

Ion Batteries

Battery Mechanism Study

Electrode Performance Exploration: Based on the micro/nano battery platform, employed in-situ electrochemical–optical microscopy and in-situ electrochemical–Raman spectroscopy to investigate the structural evolution and phase transition behaviour of electrode materials, directly linking material dynamics to device performance.

Further electrochemical measurements will be conducted to quantitatively explore the kinetics of ion insertion and extraction.

📄 Publications

XY Liu, PT Zhang, C Guan* et al.. High intrinsic phase stability of ultrathin 2M WS₂. [J]. Nat. Commun., 2024, 15:1263. (co-first authors, XY Liu and C Guan are my supervisors) doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-45676-3

The 2M-to-2H phase transition temperature increases from 120°C to 210°C in air as thickness of WS₂ is reduced from bulk to bilayer. Monolayered 1T′ WS₂ can withstand temperatures up to 350°C in air before being oxidized, and up to 450°C in argon atmosphere before transforming to 1H phase. The higher stability of thinner 2M WS₂ is attributed to stiffened intralayer bonds, enhanced thermal conductivity and higher average barrier per layer during the layer(s)-by-layer(s) phase transition process. The observed high intrinsic phase stability can expand the practical applications of ultrathin 2M TMDs.

My Contribution: Independently designed and executed all experiments, conducted complete characterization, data processing and illustration drawing, authored the manuscript draft and managed the entire submission-to-revision process.
