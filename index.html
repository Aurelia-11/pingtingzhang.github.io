Ion Battery Applications

Battery Mechanism Study

Through integrated multi-field in situ characterization, we systematically investigated the structural evolution and phase transition behavior of electrode materials to elucidate ion intercalation/deintercalation mechanisms. In situ electrochemical-optical microscopy enabled real-time observation of morphological dynamics and ion transport pathways, while synchronized Raman-electrochemical measurements provided further analysis of crystalline phase transformations and electrode-ion interactions.

XY Liu, PT Zhang, FQ Huang, C Guan* et al.. High intrinsic phase stability of ultrathin 2M WS‚ÇÇ. [J]. Nat. Commun., 2024, 15:1263. (co-first authors, XY Liu and C Guan are my supervisors) doi.org/10.1038/s41467-024-45676-3

In this study, through systematic investigation and theoretical simulation of phase change of 2M WS‚ÇÇ, we demonstrate that ultrathin 2M WS‚ÇÇ has significantly higher intrinsic thermal stability than the bulk counterparts. The 2M-to-2H phase transition temperature increases from 120¬∞C to 210¬∞C in air as thickness of WS‚ÇÇ is reduced from bulk to bilayer. Monolayered 1T‚Ä≤ WS‚ÇÇ can withstand temperatures up to 350¬∞C in air before being oxidized, and up to 450¬∞C in argon atmosphere before transforming to 1H phase. The higher stability of thinner 2M WS‚ÇÇ is attributed to stiffened intralayer bonds, enhanced thermal conductivity and higher average barrier per layer during the layer(s)-by-layer(s) phase transition process. The observed high intrinsic phase stability can expand the practical applications of ultrathin 2M TMDs.
